Section 1.4 Measurement & Units

Subsection 1.4.1 Measuring Distance

There are many ways of measuring distance. Here are a few common units of measurement for distance.

  • Inches (in)

  • Feet (ft)

  • Yards (yd)

  • Miles (mi)

  • Centimeters (cm)

  • Meters (m)

  • Kilometers (km)

Relationships Between Units.

  • There are 12 inches in 1 foot

  • There are 3 feet in 1 yard

  • There are 5280 feet in 1 mile

  • There are 100 centimeters in 1 meter

  • There are 100 meters in 1 kilometer

Example 1.4.2.

Determine an appropriate unit to measure the distance given below.

  1. Distance between Norman and Oklahoma City

  2. Height of a tall building

  3. Diameter of a cookie

  4. Length of a bug

Solution.

  1. Miles/kilometers

  2. Feet/meters

  3. Inches/centimeters

  4. Inches/centimeters

Converting between units is very important to help us conceptualize and compute information.

Subsection 1.4.2 Measuring Area

While length measures one dimensional objects, area is a measure of two-dimensional objects.

Area Formulas for Common Shapes.

  • Rectangles: Length\(\cdot\) Width

  • Triangles: \(\dfrac{1}{2}\cdot\) Length \(\cdot\) Width

  • Circles: \(\pi\cdot\)Radius\(^2\)

The units of area are similar to the units of length; we find the units for area by squaring the component length unit. Some common area units are:

  • square inches (in\(^2\))

  • square miles (mi\(^2\))

  • square meters (m\(^2\))

Subsection 1.4.3 Measuring Volume

Volume is a measure of three-dimensional objects, the three-dimensional analogue of area.

Volume Formulas for Common Shapes.

  • Rectangular prism: Length\(\cdot\)Width\(\cdot\)Height

  • Sphere: \(\dfrac{4\pi}{3}\cdot\)Radius\(^3\)

Example 1.4.8.

A new piece of luggage has a carrying capacity of 5.2 cubic feet of storage space. How many cubic inches of space is there?

Solution.

There are 8985.6 cubic inches of space.

Liquid volume is often measured with special units. Some of these are:

  • Milliliters (mL)

  • Liters (L)

  • Fluid Ounces (oz)

  • Cups (c)

  • Quarts (qt)

  • Gallons (gal)

Relationships Between Units.

  • There are 1000 milliliters in 1 liter

  • There are 8 fluid ounces in 1 cup

  • There are 4 cups in 1 quart

  • There are 4 quarts in 1 gallon

Example 1.4.9.

How many fluid ounces are in 2 gallons?

Solution.

256 fluid ounces are in 1 gallon

Subsection 1.4.4 Measuring Temperature

There are two primary units used to measure temperature: Fahrenheit and Celsius

Converting Between Fahrenheit and Celsius.

To convert from Fahrenheit to Celsius, use the formula

\begin{equation*} ^\circ C = (^\circ F-32)\cdot \dfrac{5}{9} \end{equation*}

To convert from Celsius to Fahrenheit, use the formula

\begin{equation*} ^\circ F = ^\circ C\cdot \dfrac{9}{5} + 32 \end{equation*}

A decent estimate to go from Celsius to Fahrenheit is to double the Celsius temperature, then add 32.

Example 1.4.10.

The average high in Norman in October is 75\(^\circ F\text{.}\) How high is that in Celsius?

Solution.

About 23.9\(^\circ C\)

Solution.
